Guillermo Del Toro

The King of Monsters: A fan's review.

The Monster Legacy

Guillermo del Toro is a Mexican film director, screenwriter, producer, and novelist. In his filmmaking career, Del Toro has alternated between Spanish-language dark fantasy pieces, such as the gothic horror films El espinazo del diablo (2001), and El laberinto del fauno (2006), and more mainstream American action movies, such as the vampire superhero action film, Blade II (2002), the supernatural superhero film Hellboy (2004), its sequel Hellboy II: The Golden Army (2008), science fiction monster film Pacific Rim (2013), and the gothic romance film, Crimson Peak (2015).

THE SHAPE OF WATER

Honest Review

There are so many themes in this movie. Two of the main ones are “being silenced and voiceless” and the classic “who is the real monster?” But we get a lot of other subtler themes that stem from these two. There is the theme of art/movies and how they can be a way to express yourself.

PACIFIC RIM

Honest Review

The wild story concept of giant robots versus giant monsters is very well developed and works on many levels. PACIFIC RIM is a highly entertaining science fiction movie.

PAN'S LABYRINTH

Honest Review

When people think of "fairy tales," all kinds of familiar images come to mind: from classic films such as SNOW WHITE and SLEEPING BEAUTY, to THE WIZARD OF OZ and PINOCCHIO, there is a seemingly endless array of imaginative and resonant storytelling that continues to enchant audiences both young and old. But PAN'S LABYRINTH takes a much, much more vicious approach to this kind of storytelling, and the result is a relentlessly dark, visually beautiful, and completely entrancing piece of filmmaking.
